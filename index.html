Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth
largest satellite in the Solar System and the largest
and most massive relative to it's parent planet,with
a diameter about one-quarter that of Earth

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest
in the Solar System, after Jupiter.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun.
It is named after Greek sky deity Uranus (Caelus), who in Greek mythology
is the father of Cronus (Saturn), a grandfather of Zeus (Jupiter)
and great-grandfather of Ares (Mars).

Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the Sun
and the only one in the Solar System without a
considerable atmosphere.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only
place known in the universe where life has originated and found habitability.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the third
largest and massive terrestrial object in the Solar System.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System.
It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half
times that of all the other planets
in the Solar System combined, and slightly less than
one one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.
